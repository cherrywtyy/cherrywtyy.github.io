Social Work Students

give up on their dreams

By Gong Zijun, Kwok Ka Ching, Pan Yichao,

Wong Sze Wing, Wong Ting Yan, Wong Yat Yi

The social movement against the draft amendment to the Fugitive Offenders Ordinance (hereinafter referred to as the "Anti-Extradiction Bill Movement") began in June and has lasted for more than five months. Demonstrations have become more and more fierce, and many times they evolve into conflicts between police and civilians. Social workers have been at the forefront and mediate between the police and demonstrators.

Many social work students see the contributions of front-line social workers. Therefore, they set up a street stations to cheer for Hong Kong people and to counsel people with negative emotions. Some quasi-social workers said that they also felt powerless in the "Anti-Extradiction Bill Movement". They feel that they cannot help others with the knowledge they absorbed from lessons. Their feelings of loss are difficult to resolve and doubt themselves whether they should become social workers in the future.
